Section 01 · Overview

Where your answers sit in the landscape.

Most Indian credit decisions come down to three things that compound: the fit of the product to the applicant, the quality of the bureau file, and the accuracy of the documents submitted. Your answers so far inform the first.

Why applicants get rejected

Most rejections stem from three reasons: thin or inconsistent bureau files, document mismatches, and applying to products that don't fit the applicant's current stability.

Why product fit matters

A "no" from a product you shouldn't have applied to hurts your file. A "yes" from the right product strengthens it. Fit is the decision that compounds.

Why APR isn't the whole story

Effective borrowing cost includes processing fees, insurance premiums, prepayment penalties and late charges — not just the advertised interest rate.

Why regulated lenders matter

RBI-regulated banks and NBFCs follow disclosure and grievance norms. Informal lenders do not. Verify registration before signing anything.

i

What to know first

Check your own bureau report (free once a year from CIBIL, Experian, CRIF or Equifax) before you compare offers. Your file is the single biggest factor in what you'll actually be offered.

Section 03 · Documents

Requirements and documents.

A short, practical checklist. Most delays come from missing a single line on this list.

Identity & address

  • PAN card (mandatory)
  • Aadhaar (widely used for e-KYC)
  • Utility bill or rent agreement for current address

Income proof

  • 3 months of pay slips (salaried)
  • 2 years of ITRs (self-employed)
  • 6 months of bank statements

Credit file

  • Free annual CIBIL/Experian/CRIF/Equifax report
  • Review for errors before applying anywhere
  • Dispute incorrect entries in writing

Existing debts

  • Current EMI commitments
  • Outstanding balances and closure letters
  • Any co-applicant or guarantor obligations

Section 04 · Method

How to compare the offers you'll receive.

Compare at least three options. Judge them on total repayment cost — not the monthly EMI alone.

  1. APR over headline rate. The nominal rate hides processing fees. APR includes them.
  2. Total repayment. Multiply EMI × tenure. That's what you'll actually pay.
  3. Affordability. Total EMIs across all loans should typically stay under 40 – 50% of net monthly income.
  4. Prepayment. Check foreclosure charges. Floating-rate personal loans often have none; fixed-rate often do.
  5. Hidden fees. Late fees, NACH bounce, insurance bundling — ask for the "all-in" schedule in writing.
!

Why monthly EMI alone misleads

A lower EMI with a longer tenure often means you pay more in total. Always compare total repayment — the EMI is just how the cost is sliced up.

Section 05 · Recommended next step

Before you apply, spend 10 minutes on one thing: your bureau file.

Most avoidable rejections come from surprises in the bureau report — not the applicant's income. Pull your free report, review it, and fix obvious errors before you submit anywhere.
