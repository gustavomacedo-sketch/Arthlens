Advertising Disclosure.

How advertising works on Arthlens, how it's labelled, and how we separate it from editorial content.

Why we show ads

Arthlens is free to read. To keep it free — and to fund editorial work, research and ongoing maintenance — we display advertising. Advertising is a revenue model, not an editorial input.

How ads are labelled

  • Every ad unit is framed by a visible "Advertisement" label.
  • Commercial comparison placements (if/when we add them) will carry a "Sponsored" chip and a disclosure line.
  • Sponsored articles (if/when we publish them) will carry an inline disclosure at the top of the page.

What advertisers can and cannot do

Can do: pay for ad placements, sponsor clearly labelled modules, participate in compensated comparison partnerships where applicable.

Cannot do: edit editorial content, influence the guided check logic, request favourable ranking in editorial sections, or receive advance view of unpublished articles.

Affiliate / comparison partnerships

Arthlens may, in future, receive compensation when readers choose to apply through a clearly labelled commercial comparison module. If we do:

  • The module will be labelled "Comparison partners" or similar.
  • The ordering logic and the fact of compensation will be disclosed on the page.
  • Editorial guides will remain independent of which partners appear in commercial modules.

Editorial ranking

When our editorial guides compare products or categories, the ordering reflects the guide's own argument — not payment. If that ever changes, we'll say so at the top of the affected page.

Your choices

You can accept or reject advertising cookies using the consent banner on the site. You can also change your choice at any time by clearing site data or using the preferences link.

Contact

Advertising questions: ads@arthlens.example. Editorial or accuracy concerns: editorial@arthlens.example.
